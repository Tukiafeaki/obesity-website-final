Causes of Obesity

Top 3 causes of Obesity:

Too much junk food

  • Eating too much junk food is a major cause of obesity.
  • Examples of junk food:
    • Chips
    • Candy
    • Fast food
    • Sugary drinks (soda, energy drinks)
  • Junk foods are high in calories, sugar, and fat but low in nutrients.
  • They taste good and are easy to grab, so people often eat more than needed.
  • Eating too much junk food over time can cause weight gain.
  • It’s okay to enjoy junk food sometimes, but eating it every day can make it hard to stay healthy.

    • Not Enough Exercise

  • Not getting enough physical activity is a major cause of obesity.
  • Many teens spend a lot of time sitting, such as:
    • At school
    • Doing homework
    • Watching TV
    • Scrolling on their phone
    • Playing video games
  • While it’s okay to relax sometimes, your body also needs to move.
  • Exercise helps:
    • Burn calories
    • Keep muscles and heart strong
  • Not moving enough causes the body to store extra energy from food as fat.
  • Just 30 to 60 minutes of activity each day—like walking, biking, dancing, or playing sports—can make a big difference.

    • Family History

  • Obesity can sometimes run in families.
  • If your parents or close relatives are overweight, you may be more likely to gain weight too.
  • This can be due to:
    • Genetics (traits you’re born with)
    • Family habits, such as:
      • Eating unhealthy meals
      • Not getting much exercise
  • Healthy changes can help, even if obesity runs in your family.
  • It’s not just about genes—it’s also about habits, like:
    • Cooking better meals
    • Going for walks
